# Society
## Politics & Law
- AI regulation: Governments eventually discouraged large corporations from building AGI, but it didn’t stop the average hacker. 
- Meanwhile, a case about AI personhood is working its way through the courts: a man wishes to marry his AI partner, comparing it to gay marriage. It's been heavily sensationalized, and no one really knows the true extent of the AI in question — or of the relationship. It's the closest thing the world has to a public referendum on whether AI can be a person.
- Global standing. The US has not significantly regressed despite some questionable leadership, but it is increasingly seen as behind other, more advanced nations.

## Conflict & security
- Open warfare is way more mechanized. Wars still happen, but the combatants on the battlefield are robots, at least for the side that can afford the machines.
- Terrorism is an ever-present threat, and made scarier by how completely the world now depends on technology for nearly everything. A well-placed attack on infrastructure can do damage that once required an army.
- Bioterrorism is beginning to emerge. So far the damage has been minimal and contained — but it's a growing shadow.

## Surveillance & Security Services
- Surveillance is fragmented. As more players have entered the field, the once-centralized surveillance landscape has splintered into many competing interests.
- Cat and mouse. There's a constant, metaphorical game between surveillance companies and amateur hackers who take them down using both code and magic.
- Security services are booming. Smart homes are everywhere and widely trusted, but poorly understood by the people who live in them — which has driven high demand for security services to protect (and audit) all that connected infrastructure.

## Magic users and non-magical humans
- Ordinary humans regard anyone who can use magic — mages, shapeshifters, any capacity at all — with suspicion and unease. These views have softened a lot over the years, but you’ll still find angry men yelling at clouds about how magic is one step away from demonic possession.
- At the same time, magical abilities are genuinely useful, so magic users are often needed and sought out.
- This creates a standing tension between need and fear: society depends on magic users while distrusting them. People rely on abilities while discriminating against those who have those abilities.
