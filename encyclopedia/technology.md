# Technology
## Overview
* Drones are more commonly used throughout the world
* Semi-autonomous machines, like robotic dogs, vehicles, and general-purpose robots, are widespread and increasingly ordinary to see
* Predictive AI has improved drastically and is woven into daily life
* AGI does not exist yet — as far as anyone knows.
* Smart homes are common for anyone who can afford them, and there's far less public fear about them than there is today. There's also far less understanding of what they actually do, which has consequences elsewhere.

## Machine and Artificial Intelligence
* AI is in a strange transitional phase.
* Global political and grassroots organizations have lobbied hard against the creation of fully autonomous, sapient AI agents. While this slowed the creation of bigger and better AI technologies for large corporations, the arms race never really stopped.
* Amateurs working in their basements, not constrained by government oversight, are on the frontier and pushing the boundaries of what’s possible.
* The world avoided Skynet, but whether sapient machine intelligences are already among us is not clear-cut.

## Humanoid Robots
People in high-ranking positions now routinely work alongside humanoid robots that land squarely in the uncanny valley: convincingly human-like but not, as far as we know, sapient in any meaningful sense.

## Augments & Implants
A very recent breakthrough allows some people to be augmented with internal wetware — hardware attached to the brain that grants additional abilities, including a more direct connection to the internet.

This is cutting-edge and largely untested. The downsides and long-term consequences are still unknown. The dominant supplier of this technology is [Sincline Biomedical](organizations/sincline.md), one of the largest military contractors in the field.
